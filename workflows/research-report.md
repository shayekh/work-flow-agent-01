# Workflow: Research Report

## Trigger
The user names a topic and asks for research, an analysis, or a report on it.

## Steps

1. **Clarify scope** — Before researching, ask the user:
   - Scope/angle: broad overview, or a specific question/angle to focus on?
   - Audience: confirm the default (practical, no-nonsense, jargon-free) or note a different audience.
   - What they specifically want to know — particular sub-questions, comparisons, or decisions this report should inform.
   - Depth: confirm the default short-brief format (1-2 pages) or note if they want something longer.

2. **Show a short plan** — In 2-3 bullets, state what will be searched and the themes the report will likely cover. Get implicit or explicit go-ahead before researching.

3. **Research** — Use WebSearch/WebFetch to gather information from multiple credible sources. Track each source's title and URL as it's used.

4. **Organize findings** — Group findings into 2-4 clear themes. Don't dump raw notes — synthesize.

5. **Write the report** — Follow `resources/report-template.md`. Jargon-free, bullets over paragraphs. Always include a final "Takeaways & Next Steps" section with the key takeaways and recommended next steps.

6. **Save output** — Write the report to `output/<topic-slug>-report.md`, where `<topic-slug>` is a kebab-case version of the topic.

7. **Summarize back** — Give a 1-2 sentence confirmation of what was produced and where it's saved.
